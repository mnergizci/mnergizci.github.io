About Me

Welcome to my personal website! I am a second-year PhD student at the University of Leeds. My research focuses on applying Burst Overlap Interferometry to study the Tien Shan mountain belt. Additionally, I am investigating the recent earthquakes in Turkey to determine the best North-South displacement using Sentinel-1 TOPS mode data.

Research Interests

I am interested in developing and applying advanced methods for measuring three-dimensional surface displacements using Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar (InSAR) data. These methods include Burst Overlap Interferometry (BOI), Pixel Offset Tracking (POT) and Multiple Aperture Interferometry (MAI).

Future Plans

In the future, I plan to broaden my horizons in InSAR and coding to facilitate large-scale data processing. I aim to develop algorithms and software to respond quickly to natural hazards. I am excited about the opportunity to contribute to Earth and Natural Science
