About Me

Welcome to my professional website! I am a second-year Ph.D. student at the University of Leeds, specializing in the application of Burst Overlap Interferometry to study the Tien Shan mountain belt. Additionally, my research involves investigating recent earthquakes in Turkey to determine the best North-South displacement using Sentinel-1 TOPS mode data.

Research Interests

I am dedicated to developing and applying advanced methods for measuring three-dimensional surface displacements using Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar (InSAR) data. These methods include Burst Overlap Interferometry (BOI), Pixel Offset Tracking (POT), and Multiple Aperture Interferometry (MAI).

Publications

Future Plans

In the future, I plan to broaden my skills in InSAR and coding to facilitate large-scale data processing. I want to develop algorithms and software to respond quickly to natural hazards. I am excited about the opportunity to contribute to Earth and Environmental Sciences.
